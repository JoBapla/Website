Drivers of change

Income

Right now at this moment more than 1 in 5 Vancouverites are living in poverty according to the census. Meaning that 20% of Vancouver's residents are living at or below the poverty level, which should not be happening with a city as great as ours. With Vancouver's median income reporting at $65,454 taking away cost of shelter, food, childcare, food, and transportation leaves people living in the city of Vancouver with only 5% of their income left over. Outside of the city of Vancouver people are at a much more comfortable leftover of 23% of their total income. On top of that Vancouver is known for being Canada’s highest city for having debt to income rate at 242%. The city is becoming more and more expensive and as costs keep rising with little to no income growth people will be forced to move out to a more affordable city. We need to find a way to give the citizen a higher income or reduce costs.
