Drivers of change

Displacement

The current situation with the housing and income is causing a shift in the demographic living in the city. The rising costs of living has caused residents to move out of the city, and with the rising costs of living happening at the same time as the income disparity, more and more Vancouver residents are being forced to move out of the city they grew up in. The growing costs have forced people out of Vancouver into nearby cities like Surrey, New Westminster and Burnaby where rent and living costs are cheaper in comparison to Vancouver. The problem not only affects individuals and private properties but also businesses, the cost of rent for businesses has forced some businesses to move out of the city. With these in mind, we need to find a way to protect Vancouver’s citizens and local businesses from being forced out of the city. In addition we must find a way to help protect the next generation from being forced out of their hometown.
