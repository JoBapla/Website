Drivers of change

Housing

The housing market in Vancouver is currently at an all time high! The cost of a house or rent has substantially increased and highly outweighs the increase in income over the last 10 years. The average 1 bedroom rent has increased 30% in 10 years while income has only gone up by 12% meaning that the Vancouver people have less money left over after trying to afford their housing. Increase in The cost of housing is so high that people will have to save up until their mid thirties to afford a decent house. Currently over half of all Vancouverites rent with a percentage at about 53%. Another shocking fact is that a single detached family home has increased as much as it did from 1981 to 2005 in the past 6 months percentage wise. The housing markets rise because the appeal of Vancouver is so high which is a good thing but there must be a compromise to help control and level these frighteningly high numbers.
